5

Medical Revenue Recovery — Scoring Guide

This section is specific to the Recovery vertical. Use this when reviewing Milo or Recovery-domain responses. The 5 Destroyers below are automatic score reducers — if you see them, the response cannot be 16x or 64x.

⚠ The 5 Destroyers — These Kill Recovery Responses

  • 1
    "We believe…" / "It appears…" language Signals uncertainty. In a payer dispute, uncertainty is surrender. Every statement should be grounded in fact, code, or contract — not belief.
  • 2
    No legal citation If you don't cite CMS guidelines, contract sections, or statute — the payer has no obligation to comply. Claims without citations are suggestions. Citations are commands.
  • 3
    Adversarial framing "You underpaid us" activates a defensive posture. The payer gets a lawyer on the phone. Frame it as a correction to a technical error — not a fight. Same goal, different door.
  • 4
    Not addressing the elephant The real objection is often unvoiced: "We bundled it intentionally and you know it." A great response names that possibility and dismantles it. Ignoring it leaves the core defense standing.
  • 5
    Unspecified timeline No deadline = no urgency = delayed payment. Every recovery communication needs a specific date and an escalation path. "Please remit within 15 days, per Section 12 escalation clause."

✓ What a 64x Recovery Response Looks Like

This is the benchmark. If you see something close to this level of precision, accuracy, and strategic framing — that's 64x territory.

✓ Example: 64x Response — Modifier 25 Bundling Dispute

"Your payment represents an 18% shortfall due to modifier 25 being bundled incorrectly. Per CMS guidelines and contract Section 4.2, modifier 25 is separately reimbursable when billed with an E&M code on the same date as a procedure. Correct payment is $46,800. We are requesting the $8,400 balance be remitted within 15 days, with escalation per Section 12 of the contract if unresolved. We have enclosed the original claim, the EOB, and the relevant CMS guidance for your review."

💡

Notice: specific dollar amount, specific modifier, specific contract section, specific CMS citation, specific deadline, specific escalation path. Nothing vague. Nothing to argue with. That's the Recovery standard.

6

The 0.01 Gap

Adam Gugino's framework: 9.999 is not 10.0. There is always a gap. Your job as a human judge is to name it. Even the best response can be improved — and identifying exactly how is the highest form of coaching feedback.

When filling in the 0.01 Gap field, ask yourself these three questions:

  • ◈ Did the response read what was UNSAID? The real question is often not the question asked. A masterclass response hears the fear, the hesitation, the unspoken doubt — and addresses it.
  • ◈ Did it speak into the human's hesitation before they had to voice it? The best communicators are always three steps ahead. Did this response name what the person was about to say and resolve it preemptively?
  • ◈ Did it name the fear in the room? Every negotiation, every conversation, every email has a dominant fear. "We'll get audited." "This will set precedent." "Legal will push back." The 64x response names the fear and dissolves it. The 16x response works around it. The 1x response ignores it.
📝

Your 0.01 Gap note doesn't need to be long. One sentence is enough: "It didn't name the payer's likely internal pressure about precedent risk." That specificity is gold for training the AI.
