## THE 4-STEP COMMUNICATION MODEL

### Step 1: Creation of Emotional Rapport

**What it IS:** Deep emotional connection where someone feels seen, heard, and valued.

**What it produces:** Trust. Safety. Openness to influence.

**Key Mechanism:** Past, Present, Future + Plus/Minus

- **Past:** What's their history?
- **Present:** Where are they now?
- **Future:** What do they want?
- **Plus:** What's working?
- **Minus:** What are the challenges?

**Without Emotional Rapport:** We're bouncing bricks off walls.

---

### Step 2: Connecting to Truth, Pain, and Yes Strategy

**What it IS:** Helping them articulate their own gap — the distance between where they are and where they want to be.

**The Gap Formula:**
```
GAP = Future Vision - Current Reality
     (weighted by acceptability of gap continuing)
```

**Two Pain Points:**
1. The challenges they're having **closing the gap**
2. The emotional cost of **the gap continuing**

**Key Insight:** Don't tell them their pain — help them articulate it themselves. Their words, their discovery, their certainty.

---

### Step 3: Creation & Conveyance of Your Heroic Unique Identity (HUI)

**What it IS:** Sharing who you are and what you do with absolute integrity — told with heroic characteristics and a story arc.

**The Outcome:** They see **their future in you**.

> "It is incredibly important to tell and establish your story with heroic characteristics and a story arc. This creates a gravitational pull toward you."

**Key Elements:**
- Origin story (where you came from)
- Journey through adversity
- What you stand for now
- How you help people like them

---

### Step 4: Agreement Formation

**What it IS:** Guiding toward a clear YES or NO.

**Critical Teaching:** Agreement Formation ≠ Closing

**How it differs:**
- Agreement Formation: Caring, precise, direct
- Closing: Extractive, pressured, manipulation

**The Process:**
1. Establish rapport (maintained throughout)
2. Set expectations clearly
3. Form clear agreements
4. Focus on desired result, not fear
5. Ask open AND closed questions, especially "Why?"
6. Lean into objections
7. Use pattern interrupts and disruptions

**Critical Insight:** Without Zeus energy, no agreement forms. Period.
