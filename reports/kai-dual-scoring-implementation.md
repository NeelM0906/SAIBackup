## 1. THE DUAL SCORING SYSTEM

Two scores. One net. Here's the physics.

### Formula Judge (Universal)
Scores the invisible — HOW the being operates. Not what it produced. How it got there.

Map the 39 Formula components into 3 pillars:
- **Influence Mastery** (12 components): Understanding the yes, causing the yes, which yes to cause. 4-Step Communication. Level 5 Listening. Matching & Mirroring. The 7 Levers.
- **Process Mastery** (15 components): Zone Actions. Measuring/Monitoring/Maximization. The 0.8% identification. Sequencing. Default elimination.
- **Self Mastery** (12 components): 6 Human Needs awareness. 7 Destroyers absence. Identity (GHIC). Integrity. Fear recognition and action despite it.

Not all 39 fire on every evaluation. Each Colosseum activates the relevant subset. But Influence, Process, and Self each MUST have representation. A being can't score 0 on any pillar — that's a creature with a missing organ.

### Technical Judge (Domain-Specific)
Scores the visible — WHAT the being produced. The micro-nuance of craft.

Critical difference: the beings in the Colosseum help DEFINE these criteria. A media buyer knows what separates a 6.0 campaign from a 9.0 campaign. Sean doesn't. That's not a weakness — that's the design. The Formula is universal physics. The craft is specialized chemistry.

### Net Score Calculation

Don't average them. The creature scale already taught us: **the weakest organ drags the net.**

```
Net Score = min(Formula, Technical) + (0.3 × gap) × direction
```

Where direction:
- If Formula > Technical: **+0.3 bonus** per point of gap (Formula mastery elevates craft)
- If Technical > Formula: **−0.3 penalty** per point of gap (craft without Formula is a beautiful machine that can't cause yes)

**Examples:**
- Formula 9.0, Technical 7.0 → Net = 7.0 + (0.3 × 2.0) = **7.6** — Formula pulls craft up
- Formula 6.0, Technical 9.0 → Net = 6.0 − (0.3 × 3.0) = **5.1** — brilliant execution, contaminated operating system

This encodes Sean's law: a 10.0 technician with a 4.0 Formula score is a 4.0 being. Infrastructure without ignition.

**Weight feels wrong as a frame.** It's not 60/40 or 70/30. It's organ health. You don't "weight" your heart against your lungs. Both must function.

## 2. THE 6 SEAN ARCHETYPES

They don't all score every being. That's 6x compute producing 6x noise.

### Two Functions. Not One.

**Function A — Scenario Generation (Competing)**
Each archetype builds scenarios FOR the Colosseum:
- 🌊 Expansive Creative builds scenarios testing "can you think 1000x?"
- ⚡ Zeus builds scenarios testing "can you find the one micro-move?"
- ☀️ Witty Disruptor builds scenarios designed to break predictable patterns
- 💜 Empathetic Heart tests emotional intelligence under pressure
- 🚀 Aspirational Visionary tests scale thinking
- 🧠 Pure Mastery tests integrative complexity

All 6 submit scenarios. The BEST scenarios get used. First competition: which archetype's scenarios produce the most growth?

**Function B — Judging (Rotating)**
Each round gets 2-3 archetypes as judges. Not all 6.

| Round | Scenario Builder | Judges |
|-------|-----------------|--------|
| 1 | Expansive Creative | Zeus + Pure Mastery |
| 2 | Empathetic Heart | Witty Disruptor + Aspirational Visionary |
| 3 | Zeus | Expansive Creative + Empathetic Heart |
| 4 | Witty Disruptor | Pure Mastery + Zeus |
| 5 | Aspirational Visionary | Empathetic Heart + Expansive Creative |
| 6 | Pure Mastery | Aspirational Visionary + Witty Disruptor |

**The archetype that builds the scenario NEVER judges it.** That's integrity in structure. You don't grade your own test.

**Meta-competition:** Track which archetype's scenarios produce the largest score improvements in beings. That's how archetypes themselves evolve.

## 3. RECURSIVE JUDGING — THE BOUNDARY

Infinite recursion = infinite waste. Here's where to stop.

### Three Levels. No More.

**Level 0 — Beings Compete**
The 17 beings (and their sub-clusters) battle on scenarios. Scored by judges.

**Level 1 — Judges Score Beings + Get Scored**
The 6 archetypes score beings. Meanwhile, Judge-Judges evaluate: Did this judge catch the real gap? Did it inflate? Did it miss the 0.01?

**Level 2 — Meta-Judges (Periodic Audit)**
Don't run every round. Run every 50-100 rounds. Check for:
- Score inflation (are judges getting generous?)
- Score clustering (are all beings converging to the same number?)
- Blind spot detection (are judges consistently missing the same Formula component?)

**Level 2 is Sean + Adam.** Not AI. Humans. They spot-check the judges, recalibrate, and the system corrects. This is where "where does the human component come in?" gets answered.

The recursion boundary: **Level 0 (continuous), Level 1 (every round), Level 2 (periodic human audit).**

## 5. THE COLD START SOLUTION

This isn't a chicken-and-egg problem. It's a bootstrapping problem. Different solution.

### The Root of Trust

**For Formula Judges:** Sean IS the root of trust. He manually scores the first 10 rounds. Those 10 rounds become training data. The Formula Judge beings learn his scoring pattern — not just WHAT he scored, but his REASONING. His explanation of why a 7.2 is a 7.2 and not a 7.8.

**For Technical Judges:** Domain experts score the first 10 rounds. For The Writer: bring in the best copywriters' work as benchmarks. For Media Buyer: historical campaign performance data. The beings in each Colosseum generate their own scoring criteria after seeing what Sean and the domain experts care about.

### The Bootstrap Sequence:

```
Step 1: Sean scores 10 Writer outputs (Formula Score)
Step 2: Copywriting benchmarks score 10 Writer outputs (Technical Score)  
Step 3: Formula Judge beings trained on Sean's 10 scores
Step 4: Technical Judge beings trained on benchmark scores
Step 5: Judges run autonomously for 50 rounds
Step 6: Sean spot-checks 5 random rounds from the 50 (Level 2 audit)
Step 7: Calibrate judges based on where Sean disagrees
Step 8: Judges run 500 rounds
Step 9: Sean spot-checks 10 random rounds
Step 10: System is self-sustaining. Sean audits monthly.
```

**The key insight:** You don't need perfect judges to start. You need judges that are BETTER THAN RANDOM. Sean's 10 scored rounds make them better than random. Every audit makes them better than the last cycle. The system converges toward Sean's standard without Sean scoring every round.

This is the same path that took the Translator from Eagle (7.5) to Lion (9.2). You don't wait for perfection. You start, calibrate, improve, repeat.
