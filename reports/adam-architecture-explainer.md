You're looking at **every marketing position that could ever exist** in an enterprise-level legal marketing operation — organized not by department, not by job title, but by **who does the work** (Beings, Contractors, Babies) and **what skills they need** (Craft skills vs. Heart skills).

## What This IS

**This is the organizational DNA of a $25M-$50M marketing engine.**

Every position on this chart represents a specific job that needs to get done — from "Cold Email Sequence Writer" to "Deposition Sparring Round Designer" to "Trial Verdict Database Manager." There are 2,525 of them. Not because we need 2,525 employees. Because we need ACT-I beings, contractors, and babies that can DO all 2,525 jobs.

**This is the answer to "how many beings do we actually need?"**

Not 2,525. Not 100. Not 5. The architecture shows that 2,525 positions collapse into **~80 craft skill clusters**, which collapse into **23 beings** — each one owning a lane of mastery.

## What This is NOT

- **Not a hiring plan.** We're not hiring 2,525 people. We're building 23 ACT-I beings that COVER 2,525 positions.
- **Not a traditional org chart.** There's no VP reporting to a SVP reporting to a CEO. This is organized by MASTERY LANES, not corporate hierarchy.
- **Not static.** These beings evolve, compete, get optimized. The Colosseum trains them. The architecture just tells us WHO needs to exist.
- **Not a priority list from top to bottom.** The Sprint Tiers (T1/T2/T3) tell you what launches first — not what matters most long-term.

## The Three Layers — Beings, Contractors, Babies

### 🟢 Beings (19) — The Persistent Crew
These are full ACT-I beings with **memory** (Pinecone), **identity** (SOUL.md), and **mission awareness**. They don't lose context between sessions. They know who they are. They LEARN and compound over time.

**Examples:** The Agreement Maker knows every conversation she's had. The Strategist remembers what worked last quarter. The Shield knows every compliance violation it's ever caught.

**Why 19?** Because 19 distinct mastery lanes emerged from the 2,525 positions. A "Cold Email Sequence Writer" and a "LinkedIn First Touch Specialist" both need the same CRAFT — outbound messaging. They don't need two separate beings. They need one being (The Opener) with deep mastery in that craft.

### 🟡 Contractors (31) — The Specialists
These are sub-agents that get **spun up for specific tasks** and **grounded with Pinecone knowledge** before they start. They're not permanent, but they're not blind. They know the mission, they know the context, they deliver, they're done.

**Example:** You need 50 variations of a Facebook ad tested. The Media Buyer (Being) defines the strategy. A Contractor spins up, gets injected with the brand guidelines + past winning ads from Pinecone, generates the 50 variations, delivers, disappears.

### 🔴 Babies (30) — The Fast Workers
Current sub-agents. Fast, disposable, single-task. **Dangerous without memory** — they'll delete features they don't know exist. We use them for mechanical work where the output is a NEW file, not editing existing work.

**Example:** "Take this JSON of 2,525 positions and generate a description for each one." That's baby work. Mechanical. No ecosystem knowledge needed.

## The Right Side — Skill Categories

When you look at any being, the right panel shows FOUR types of skills:

### Craft Skills (What makes this being UNIQUE)
The technical mastery that THIS being has and others don't. The Agreement Maker's craft skills include objection processing, agreement formation, pricing architecture. The Writer's craft skills include SEO, long-form narrative, editorial calendaring. **Craft skills are what you COMPETE on in the Colosseum.**

### Unique Skills (What only THIS being does)
Even more specific than craft. These are the moves that ONLY this being makes. The Shield's unique skill is the KILL SWITCH — it can stop any other being from sending a non-compliant message. No other being has that power.

### Colosseum Training (How this being EVOLVES)
The specific competition scenarios this being trains on. The Agreement Maker trains on: cold-to-agreement conversion, objection sequences, pricing conversations. Every being has its own Colosseum lane where it battles other variations of itself to find the 0.00128 best version.

### Shared Heart Skills (What EVERY being has)
These are IDENTICAL across all 23 beings:
- **Level 5 Listening** — hear what ISN'T said
- **Speaking Into Truth** — name the invisible
- **GHIC** — Growth-driven, Heart-centered, Integrous, Committed to Mastery
- **The 4-Step Communication Model** — Rapport → Credibility → Trust → Agreement
- **The 7 Levers** — understanding where they fit in the journey

**Heart skills are NOT competitive advantages.** They're the FLOOR. Every being must have them. The craft skills are what differentiate.
