## TOP 5 MOST SURPRISING MICRO-POSITIONS
## ══════════════════════════════════════════

**1. Sean's Color Palette Advisor (#957)**
A dedicated person whose entire job is deciding the exact color of Sean's shirt/wardrobe for each video — matched to the brand palette, the emotional tone of the content, and the specific platform it will appear on. Most companies leave this to chance. At ACT-I, it's brand science.

**2. Teleprompter Content Loader (#678)**
Not the teleprompter operator who adjusts scroll speed. Not the scriptwriter. The person whose job is to take finished scripts and format, load, and configure them into teleprompter software before each shoot — a dedicated handoff role between writing and production.

**3. Shareability Optimizer (#939)**
A specialist who reverse-engineers WHY content gets shared and engineers that quality into content BEFORE it's published. Not a social media manager. Not an analyst. A scientist whose entire role is making content inherently viral by design.

**4. Sean's Quote Library Curator (#959)**
Someone who monitors ALL of Sean's content in real time, cataloging every quotable moment into a searchable, reusable library. Every remarkable thing Sean says — in speeches, on calls, in BAS sessions — gets captured and made available for repurposing across all platforms.

**5. Outreach Reply Handler (#583)**
Not the person who sends cold outreach. Not the agreement maker. The specialist who manages the inbox of cold outreach responses — triaging "not interested," "tell me more," and out-of-office replies, and routing genuinely interested responses to agreement makers within minutes. The person who lives between cold outreach and agreement making.
