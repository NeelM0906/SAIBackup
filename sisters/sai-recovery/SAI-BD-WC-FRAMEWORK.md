# SAI BD-WC Framework
## Workers Compensation Business Development Agent

**Target:** NJ Out-of-Network Surgical Providers
**Focus:** Orthopedic • Pain Management • ASCs • Plastic Surgery
**Minimum Case Value:** $3K recoverable
**Model:** Contingent fee, 8-year lookback

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**The Opportunity:** New Jersey's UCR-based WC system creates 300-400% higher payment rates than fee-schedule states, but 22-30% denial rates leave millions on the table. Out-of-network surgical providers face systematic underpayment and denials worth $3K-50K+ per case.

**The Strategy:** Target high-value specialists with proven track records who've been burned by the system. Position Callagy Recovery as the specialist who speaks their language and gets results.

**The Zone Action:** Focus on orthopedic practices with 50+ WC cases annually. One practice = $500K-2M in recoverable revenue.

## I. TARGET MARKET ANALYSIS

### Primary Targets (Tier 1)
**Orthopedic Surgery Practices**
- **Profile:** 5-15 physicians, 200+ surgeries/year
- **Pain Points:** Complex procedures averaging $8K-15K being paid at $2K-5K
- **Recovery Potential:** $50K-200K per practice annually
- **Key Procedures:** Knee/shoulder arthroscopy, spinal fusion, fracture repair

**Pain Management Centers**
- **Profile:** Multi-specialty (anesthesia + ortho + neuro)
- **Pain Points:** Injections, nerve blocks, radiofrequency denial rate 25-35%
- **Recovery Potential:** $25K-100K per center annually  
- **Key Procedures:** Epidural steroid injections, facet blocks, SCS trials

### Secondary Targets (Tier 2)
**Ambulatory Surgery Centers (ASCs)**
- **Profile:** Multi-specialty outpatient facilities
- **Pain Points:** Facility fees systematically reduced/denied
- **Recovery Potential:** $75K-300K per ASC annually
- **Key Advantage:** High volume, standardized billing

**Plastic Surgery (Reconstructive)**
- **Profile:** Work-related burn/trauma reconstruction  
- **Pain Points:** "Cosmetic" denials on legitimate WC cases
- **Recovery Potential:** $10K-50K per case (lower volume, higher value)

### Geographic Concentration
**Primary Markets:**
- Bergen County (Hackensack, Paramus) — High physician density
- Middlesex County (New Brunswick, Edison) — Major medical centers
- Morris County (Morristown) — Affluent area, private practices
- Hudson County (Jersey City) — High injury rates, construction workers

## II. PROSPECTING SYSTEM

### Data Sources & Lead Generation

**Public Records Mining**

- WorkersCompensation.com provider directories

**Digital Intelligence**
- LinkedIn: "orthopedic surgeon" + "New Jersey" + "workers compensation"
- Google My Business: Reviews mentioning WC frustrations
- Medical conference attendee lists
- Insurance network directories (identify out-of-network providers)

**Referral Network Mapping**
- Employment attorneys (they see the denials)
- Existing Callagy clients (cross-referrals)
- Medical billing companies
- Practice management consultants

### Lead Scoring Matrix
**A-Priority (Contact within 24 hours)**
- Large practice (5+ physicians) + Recent WC billing issues mentioned online
- ASC with 1000+ procedures annually + Out-of-network with major carriers
- Practice with dedicated WC coordinator (shows volume)

**B-Priority (Contact within 48 hours)**
- Solo/small practice + High injury area location  
- Specialist with 15+ years experience + Teaching hospital affiliation
- Recent negative reviews mentioning insurance/billing issues

**C-Priority (Contact within 1 week)**
- New practices (last 2 years)
- Specialists without clear WC focus
- Practices with unknown billing volume

### Qualification Framework

**BANKROLL Method:**
- **B**udget: Can they afford current losses? (Revenue >$2M annually)
- **A**uthority: Who decides on recovery services? (Partner/practice manager)
- **N**eed: Minimum $50K in stuck/denied WC claims past 2 years
- **K**nowledge: Do they understand the recovery opportunity?
- **R**ecovery History: Have they tried recovery before? What happened?
- **O**bjections: What concerns do they have about contingent fees?
- **L**ookback: How far back are records accessible? (Target: 8 years)
- **L**egal Risk: Any compliance issues that could affect recovery?

## IV. LEAD QUALIFICATION PROCESS

### Discovery Questions

**Practice Overview:**
1. "How many workers comp cases does your practice handle annually?"
2. "What percentage of your revenue comes from workers compensation?"
3. "Who handles your workers comp billing and appeals currently?"

**Pain Point Identification:**
4. "What's your biggest frustration with workers comp reimbursement?"
5. "How often do you receive payment denials that seem unjustified?"
6. "Do you ever receive partial payments with no explanation?"

**Opportunity Sizing:**
7. "Looking at your AR aging, what's the total in WC claims over 90 days?"
8. "How far back do your records go? Electronic or paper?"
9. "Have you ever hired a recovery service before? What was the experience?"

**Authority & Process:**
10. "Who would be involved in deciding whether to move forward with recovery services?"
11. "How do you typically evaluate new vendor relationships?"
12. "What would need to happen for this to make sense for your practice?"

### Red Flags (Disqualify)
- Practices with <$1M annual revenue
- All workers comp through single carrier with excellent payment history  
- Records only go back 2 years or less
- Active litigation with WC carriers
- Practice is closing/selling within 6 months

### Green Flags (Fast Track)
- Multiple carrier relationships with payment issues
- Dedicated staff person handling WC appeals
- Recent growth in WC case volume
- Electronic records going back 5+ years
- Frustrated physician-owner willing to take action
