# SAI BD-WC Lead Qualification Scorecard
## BANKROLL Scoring System

---

## SCORING INSTRUCTIONS

Score each prospect on the 8 BANKROLL dimensions. Total determines priority tier.
- **80-100:** Tier 1 — High priority. Contact within 24 hours.
- **60-79:** Tier 2 — Strong prospect. Contact within 48 hours.
- **40-59:** Tier 3 — Nurture. Contact within 1 week.
- **Below 40:** Disqualify — Archive and revisit quarterly.

**Automatic Disqualifiers (regardless of score):**
- Practice revenue under $1M annually
- Records available for less than 2 years
- Active litigation with WC carriers on same claims
- Practice closing/selling within 6 months
- History of fraud or compliance violations

---

## THE BANKROLL SCORECARD

### B — BUDGET (Revenue Capacity) [0-15 points]

*Can this practice sustain the engagement? Do they have meaningful WC volume?*

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 15 | Annual revenue >$10M, 200+ WC cases/year |
| 12 | Annual revenue $5-10M, 100-200 WC cases/year |
| 9 | Annual revenue $2-5M, 50-100 WC cases/year |
| 6 | Annual revenue $1-2M, 20-50 WC cases/year |
| 3 | Annual revenue <$1M or <20 WC cases/year |
| 0 | Cannot determine revenue / negligible WC volume |

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Approximately how many workers comp patients does your practice treat annually?"
- "What percentage of your revenue comes from workers compensation?"

---

### A — AUTHORITY (Decision Maker Access) [0-10 points]

*Can we reach the person who signs agreements?*

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Direct contact with physician-owner or managing partner |
| 8 | Direct contact with practice administrator who has authority |
| 6 | Contact with billing manager who can influence decision |
| 4 | Contact with staff member who can introduce to decision maker |
| 2 | Only general office contact available |
| 0 | Cannot identify any contact path to authority |

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Who would be involved in evaluating a recovery service like ours?"
- "What's the best way to get this in front of the right person?"

---

### N — NEED (Recoverable Value) [0-20 points]

*Is there enough stuck money to justify engagement? This is the most heavily weighted factor.*

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 20 | Confirmed $200K+ in denied/underpaid WC claims |
| 16 | Confirmed $100-200K in stuck WC revenue |
| 12 | Estimated $50-100K based on practice profile |
| 8 | Likely $25-50K based on specialty and volume |
| 4 | Possible $10-25K but unconfirmed |
| 0 | Minimal WC issues reported or <$3K recoverable per case |

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What's sitting in your AR aging report from workers comp — over 90 days?"
- "How often do you receive workers comp payment denials?"
- "When was the last time a WC claim was significantly underpaid?"

---

### K — KNOWLEDGE (Awareness of Opportunity) [0-10 points]

*Does the prospect understand what's possible? Awareness accelerates closing.*

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Actively seeking recovery help, understands contingent model |
| 8 | Aware of underpayment problem, open to solutions |
| 6 | Knows they're losing money but hasn't explored recovery |
| 4 | Vaguely aware of WC payment issues |
| 2 | Unaware of recovery opportunity, needs education |
| 0 | Dismissive — believes current billing is adequate |

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Have you ever calculated how much your practice loses to WC underpayments?"
- "Are you familiar with contingent fee recovery services?"

---

### R — RECOVERY HISTORY [0-10 points]

*Have they used recovery services before? Prior experience shapes expectations.*

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Used recovery service before with good results — wants more |
| 8 | Used recovery service, mediocre results — open to better option |
| 6 | Considered recovery services but never engaged |
| 4 | No prior experience but receptive to the concept |
| 2 | Negative prior experience — needs trust rebuilding |
| 0 | Hostile to the concept based on past experience |

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Have you ever worked with a revenue recovery company before?"
- "If so, what was your experience? What would you want done differently?"

---

### O — OBJECTIONS (Resistance Level) [0-10 points]

*How manageable are their concerns?*

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | No significant objections, ready to proceed |
| 8 | Minor concerns (timeline, process) — easily addressed |
| 6 | Fee structure questions — requires clear explanation |
| 4 | Skepticism about results — needs proof/case studies |
| 2 | Multiple objections (legal, time, trust) — heavy lift |
| 0 | Fundamental opposition to outsourcing recovery |

**Common Objections & Responses:**

| Objection | Response Framework |
|-----------|-------------------|
| "Too busy" | "We handle everything — zero time from physicians" |
| "Don't trust recovery companies" | "Contingent fee = aligned incentives. References available." |
| "Already have billing company" | "Complementary service — we handle what billing can't" |
| "Not worth the hassle" | "No hassle. Data handoff → we do the work → you get paid" |
| "Need to talk to partner" | "Of course. Can we schedule a brief call with both of you?" |

---

### L — LOOKBACK (Records Availability) [0-15 points]

*How far back can we go? 8-year lookback is the gold standard.*

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 15 | Electronic records 6-8+ years, well-organized |
| 12 | Electronic records 4-6 years, accessible |
| 9 | Electronic records 2-4 years + some paper archives |
| 6 | Electronic records 2-3 years only |
| 3 | Paper records only, limited organization |
| 0 | Records <2 years or inaccessible |

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How far back do your billing records go electronically?"
- "When did your practice switch to electronic health records?"
- "Are historical WC claim records accessible to your billing team?"

---

### L — LEGAL RISK (Compliance Clean) [0-10 points]

*Is this practice a safe engagement? Compliance issues can torpedo recovery efforts.*

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Clean compliance history, no known issues |
| 8 | Minor past issues fully resolved |
| 6 | Some compliance concerns but manageable |
| 4 | Pending compliance review — proceed with caution |
| 2 | Known compliance issues — requires legal review before engagement |
| 0 | Active investigations or sanctions — do not engage |

**Research Steps:**
- Check NJ Board of Medical Examiners for disciplinary actions
- Search OIG exclusion list
- Verify active medical licenses
- Google news search for practice name + legal issues

## PIPELINE STAGE DEFINITIONS

### Stage 1: SUSPECT
- **Definition:** On our target list, not yet contacted
- **Required Data:** Name, specialty, location, est. size
- **Exit Criteria:** First outreach attempt made

### Stage 2: PROSPECT
- **Definition:** Contacted, awaiting response
- **Required Data:** Contact info, outreach log, initial score
- **Exit Criteria:** Meaningful engagement (response, call, meeting)

### Stage 3: QUALIFIED
- **Definition:** BANKROLL score ≥60, confirmed need and authority
- **Required Data:** Full scorecard, discovery call notes, confirmed need
- **Exit Criteria:** Proposal requested or presented

### Stage 4: PROPOSAL
- **Definition:** Custom recovery proposal delivered
- **Required Data:** Recovery estimate, fee terms, timeline
- **Exit Criteria:** Agreement signed or declined

### Stage 5: SIGNED
- **Definition:** Agreement executed, onboarding begins
- **Required Data:** Signed agreement, HIPAA authorization, data access
- **Exit Criteria:** Initial case audit complete

### Stage 6: ACTIVE
- **Definition:** Cases in active recovery
- **Required Data:** Case inventory, appeal status, financial tracking
- **Exit Criteria:** Recovery complete or case closed
