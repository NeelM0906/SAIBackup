## SPECIALTY-SPECIFIC PROSPECTING GUIDES

### Orthopedic Practices — What to Look For
**High-Value Indicators:**
- Practice advertises "workers compensation accepted"
- Multiple fellowship-trained surgeons (spine, sports, hand, joint)
- Affiliated ASC (own facility = higher charges = higher recovery)
- Located near industrial/construction corridors
- Employs physician assistants / NPs (indicates volume)

**Research Signals:**
- Google reviews mentioning "work injury" or "workers comp"
- Website with dedicated WC page or injury types
- Physician listed as IME (Independent Medical Examiner) provider
- Presentations at WC-related conferences
- Membership in AAOS, AOA, or state ortho societies

**Typical Recoverable Procedures:**

### Pain Management — What to Look For
**High-Value Indicators:**
- Board-certified in Pain Medicine AND Anesthesiology
- Multiple procedure rooms or in-office fluoroscopy suite
- High volume of injection procedures
- Treats chronic pain from workplace injuries
- Sees patients referred from orthopedic surgeons

**Typical Recoverable Procedures:**

### Ambulatory Surgery Centers — What to Look For
**High-Value Indicators:**
- Multi-specialty (ortho + pain + general surgery)
- >1,000 cases annually
- Out-of-network with major WC carriers
- Physician-owned (stronger motivation to recover)
- Located in high-density physician area

**Typical Recoverable Charges:**
| Service | Avg Billed | Avg WC Paid | Recovery Gap |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Facility fee — knee arthroscopy | $15,000 | $5,000 | $10,000 |
| Facility fee — rotator cuff repair | $18,000 | $6,000 | $12,000 |
| Facility fee — spinal injection | $6,000 | $2,000 | $4,000 |
| Facility fee — carpal tunnel release | $8,000 | $3,000 | $5,000 |
| Facility fee — spinal fusion | $45,000 | $15,000 | $30,000 |

### Plastic Surgery (Reconstructive) — What to Look For
**High-Value Indicators:**
- Specializes in hand/microsurgery or burn reconstruction
- Hospital-affiliated trauma reconstruction
- Treats industrial/construction injuries
- Fellowship in hand/upper extremity surgery
- Located near industrial zones or trauma centers

## REFERRAL PARTNER DATABASE

### Employment / Workers Comp Attorneys
**Why They Refer:**
- See denied claims firsthand
- Their clients need treatment but providers won't accept WC without payment assurance
- Successful recovery improves their case outcomes

**How to Find:**
- NJ State Bar Association Workers Compensation Section
- NJAJ (NJ Association for Justice) directory
- LinkedIn: "workers compensation attorney" + NJ
- Martindale-Hubbell directory

### Medical Billing Companies
**Why They Refer:**
- Complex WC recovery is outside their core capability
- They want to keep their clients happy
- Revenue share opportunity on referred recoveries

**How to Find:**
- HBMA (Healthcare Billing & Management Association)
- LinkedIn: "medical billing" + "workers compensation" + NJ
- Billing company directories
- Existing Callagy client billing companies

### Practice Management Consultants
**Why They Refer:**
- Help practices improve revenue
- Recovery services enhance their value proposition
- Ongoing consulting relationship deepens trust
