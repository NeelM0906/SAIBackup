## Key Insights from Research

### New Jersey PIP Environment
- **Legal requirement:** 60-day payment (+ 45-day extension allowed)
- **Reality:** 6-12 month delays common
- **Provider pain:** $50K-$200K outstanding receivables per practice
- **Recovery opportunity:** Interest penalties, arbitration rights

### New York PIP Environment
- **Legal requirement:** 30-day payment
- **Massive advantage:** 2% monthly interest = 24% annually
- **Arbitration backlog:** 10-month AAA wait (but interest accrues entire time!)
- **Settlement strategy:** Aggressive pre-arbitration pursuit yields 60-day recoveries

### Target Pain Points
- **Emergency medicine:** High auto accident volume, low reimbursement priority from insurers
- **Orthopedic practices:** Complex cases = higher denial rates (medical necessity challenges)
- **Administrative burden:** Internal staff overwhelmed by PIP appeals and follow-ups
- **Cash flow crisis:** Outstanding receivables straining practice operations
