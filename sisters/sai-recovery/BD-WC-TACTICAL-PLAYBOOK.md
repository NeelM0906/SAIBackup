## WEEK 3: CONVERSION FOCUS

### Qualified Lead Management
**Immediate Response Protocol:**
- Within 2 hours: Send calendar booking confirmation + prep materials
- Within 24 hours: Research their practice thoroughly (website, news, reviews)  
- Before call: Prepare customized value proposition based on their size/specialty

**Discovery Call Structure (30 minutes):**
**Minutes 1-5: Rapport & Context**
- Thank them for their time
- Brief overview of Callagy Recovery credentials
- Confirm what they know about revenue recovery

**Minutes 6-15: Discovery**
- Practice overview (size, WC volume, current billing setup)
- Pain point identification (specific examples of denials/underpayments)
- Quantification (estimated stuck revenue, timeframes)

**Minutes 16-25: Solution Positioning**
- How we work (contingent fee, no risk explanation)
- Similar case examples (social proof)
- Timeline and process overview  

**Minutes 26-30: Next Steps**
- Qualify interest level ("Is this something you'd want to move forward with?")
- Address concerns/objections
- Schedule follow-up or proposal presentation

### Objection Handling Scripts

**"We don't have much workers comp"**
*"I understand. Even practices with 20-30 cases annually often have significant recovery opportunities. It's not about volume—it's about the systematic patterns we see. Would it be worth 10 minutes to review your AR aging report together?"*

**"Our billing company handles collections"**
*"That's exactly right—billing companies are great at initial claims and standard collections. We focus specifically on the complex denials and underpayments that even excellent billing companies can't resolve. It's complementary expertise, not competing services."*

**"What are your fees?"**
*"We work on a pure contingency basis. You pay absolutely nothing unless we actually recover money for your practice. Our fee is a percentage of what we collect—and it's structured so you always receive more than we do. Would you like me to walk through how that works specifically?"*

**"We need to think about it"**
*"Of course—this is a business decision that deserves consideration. Help me understand what specific questions or concerns you have so I can address them directly. Is it the fee structure, the time commitment, or something else?"*
