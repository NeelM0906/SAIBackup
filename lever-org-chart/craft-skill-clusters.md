## Hard-to-Classify Positions

Several positions sit at craft skill boundaries:

1. **Content managers by vertical** (e.g., "Healthcare Content Manager", "Financial Content Manager") — classified as Content Strategy because the craft is editorial management, not domain expertise
2. **Platform-specific social media roles** (e.g., "TikTok Content Creator" vs "LinkedIn Content Manager") — kept in Social Media Management because the craft (platform algorithm mastery, community engagement) is the same despite different platforms
3. **"Director of" roles within divisions** — classified by their CRAFT, not their seniority. A "Director of Video Marketing" goes into Video Operations because that is the work.
4. **Sales analysts vs. marketing analysts** — separated because the data they analyze and the decisions they inform are fundamentally different crafts
5. **Partnership roles** span multiple craft skills (sourcing is like outreach, agreement-making is like sales, nurturing is like account management) — kept as distinct partnership clusters because the CONTEXT (ecosystem mergers) makes the craft genuinely different

### Key Insight

The blog writer and the book writer share Level 5 Listening and Speaking Into Truth (HEART skills). But the blog writer needs **SEO keyword research, meta descriptions, internal linking** while the book writer needs **narrative arc, chapter structure, sustained argument over 200 pages**. These are genuinely different crafts.

Similarly, a social media writer (hashtag strategy, character limits, platform tone) and an email writer (subject lines, preview text, sequence architecture) share the HEART of persuasion but exercise fundamentally different CRAFT muscles.

The 81 clusters in this report represent the **true craft skill boundaries** — where the skill genuinely diverges, not where the platform or lever changes.
